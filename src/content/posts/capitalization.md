i don't like current capitalization conventions. 

i find it super unnecessary to have to capitalize the first letter of a sentence. some platforms do have auto-capitalization, but for the ones that don't, i think it's so worth the slight optimization to not have to press the shift button on every sentence.

i also don't like the capitalization rules for referencing proper nouns. "capitalize the first letter of every word in the proper noun. words like 'the' and 'a' are not capitalized unless they are the first word." i propose that we just capitalize every letter in the proper noun. this way there is no confusion on what should be capitalized. plus, usually proper nouns are very important pieces of information in a text so we want them to stand out anyway.

let me give you an example.

"My favorite book is Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire."

compared to 

"my favorite book is HARRY POTTER AND THE GOBLET OF FIRE."

the actual subject of the sentence stands out so much more and it took me so much less time to type out.

another edge case optimization with this approach is that you will never confuse the lowercase "l" with the uppercase "I". see, as you read that sentence, you only knew which letter was which because i defined them as being lowercase and uppercase letters. for all you know, i could've typed the uppercase "i" for the first one and the lowercase "l" for the second one. some fonts do have the little platforms at the top and bottom of the "i", but for the ones that don't, this approach remedies that confusion.
